The Movie

1 .The Film

The shoot was infamously troubled, prolonged by the difficulties of working at sea and the malfunctions of the mechanical shark. Yet jaws is a classic piece of storytelling, combining horror, adventure and the character driven tale of na outsider hero who must combat the community and his own fear of water. The narrative concerns the disruption of lives and livelihoods on the island resort of Amity, after a great white shark sets up camp in its waters. After it takes a bite out of the Fourth of july, police chief Mar tins Brody (Roy Scheider) sets out to sea on board the Orca, with its ferocious Captain Quint (Robert Shaw), and the oceanographer Matt Hooper (Richard Dreyfuss), to kill Amitys tormentor.

2 . The Shooting

Jaws held early signs of Spielbergs pure cinematic instinct, from the use of the animals point of view to heighten suspense, to the foward tracking, zoom-out shot when brody realizes it has eaten a boy in front of his eyes. However, the film's success would be unthinkable without John William's soundtrack, founded on na omnous two note motif for the shark, Verna Fields editing and the actors performances. Robert Shaw's Ahab-like fisherman is the most memorable, although it was Roy Scheider improvisation that gave Jaws its best line: Youre gonna need a bigger boat. DMM

3 . The Soundtrack

The film that invented the summer blockbuster and the first to break the famed US $ 100 million mark at the box office was a genuine phenomenon. In the summer of 1975, everyone was talking about Jaws. The movie destroyed the swimming habits of a generation and propelled the career of a gifted young director into the stratosphere.

Key Scenes

1. The night feeding

Chrissie leaves a beach party with a boy for a moonlight swim. He fumbles with his clothes while she heads out to the sea. Suddenly she is tugged beneath the water, then dragged across the surface, screaming, before being pulled under. A heart stopping opening.

2. The fourth of July

Amity's mayor opens the beaches for the fourth of July holiday. There is a false alarm, panicking hundreds of swimmers. Meanwhile, the shark enters the pond where Brody's son Michael and his friends are boating. The shark kills a man who tries to help them.

3. The Indianapolis

Quint relates his formative experience in World War II, when the USS indianapolis was torpedoed and sunk. He describes days spent in the water, as sharks feasted on his shipmates. It is Spielberg's favourite scene, the author of its final draft is debated.

4. Killing the shark

Brody is alone on the sinking boat as the shark, having killed Quint, attacks again. He throws na air tank into its mouth, then climbs on to the mast with a rifle and fires at the tank. It explodes, blowing the animal to pieces, Hooper surfaces and the two men swim to shore.

Director Profile

1946-69

Steven Spielberg was born in 1946. After failing to get into the University of Southern California. He worked as na unpaid intern at Universal Studios. He directed his first short film Amblin' in 1968. Its success led to a seven year contract with Universal.

1970-74

He directed for US TV, including episodes of Marcus Welby, MD and Columbo, but most significantly the TV movie Duel (1971). He made his cinema debut with the Sugarland Express (1974).

1983-92

A relatively dry period for Spielberg saw Indiana Jones sequels and poorly received attempts at serious fare, such as The Color Purple (1985). He enjoyed commercial success as producer of Back to the Future (1985) and Who Framed Roger Rabbit (1988).

1993

While jurassic Park spearheaded the use of CGI and made US $900 million, Holocaust drama Schindler's List picked up seven Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Spielberg's first for Best Director.

1994 - Present

Spielberg established the Shoah Foundation, to record video testimonies of Holocaust survivors, and co founded a new Hollywood studio, DreamWorks, Saving Private Ryan (1998) saw him pick up another Oscar for Best Director. He continued to direct a wide range of films. From sci-fi (Minority Report, 2005; Ready Player One, 2019), family movies (The BFG, 2016) and real life dramas (Bridge of Spies, 2015: The Post, 2017).
